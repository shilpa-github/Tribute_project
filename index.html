Tribute To Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla

1961 - 2003

About Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla was the first woman of Indian origin in space. She first flew on Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 as a mission specialist and primary robotic arm operator. In 2003, she was one of the seven crew members who tragically died in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster.

Chawla's achievements and contributions to aerospace science have inspired millions around the world, especially in India.

Key Achievements

  • First woman of Indian origin in space
  • Primary robotic arm operator during her first flight
  • Recipient of several honors and awards posthumously

Tribute

Kalpana Chawla's life and work continue to inspire young women and men across the globe. Her dedication to space exploration and her courage in the face of adversity serve as a beacon of hope and determination.

Her legacy lives on in the hearts of all those who dare to dream and reach for the stars.
